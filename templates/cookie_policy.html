Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit MindFlow. They help us remember your preferences, keep you signed in, understand how you use our platform, and improve your experience. Some cookies are essential for the platform to function; others are optional and require your consent.

Types of Cookies We Use

🔒
Essential Cookies Always Active

Required for core platform functionality — authentication, security, session management, and CSRF protection. These cannot be disabled without breaking the platform.

⚙️
Functional Cookies Optional

Remember your preferences such as language, theme, notification settings, and accessibility options to provide a personalized experience.

📊
Analytics Cookies Optional

Help us understand how users interact with MindFlow so we can improve the platform. All analytics data is anonymized and aggregated — never personally identifiable.

🚫
Marketing Cookies Not Used

MindFlow does not use advertising or marketing cookies. We do not track users for advertising purposes or share data with ad networks.

Third-Party Cookies

We use a minimal set of trusted third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Cloudflare: Security and DDoS protection cookies (essential).
  • Stripe: Payment processing cookies, only on billing pages (essential).
  • Plausible Analytics: Privacy-first analytics — no cross-site tracking, no fingerprinting, fully GDPR compliant (optional, opt-in).

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any advertising network cookies.

How to Manage Cookies

You can control cookies through our preference center (below), or through your browser settings. Note that disabling essential cookies will prevent MindFlow from functioning correctly.

  • Google Chrome — cookies settings
  • Mozilla Firefox — cookies settings
  • Apple Safari — cookies settings
