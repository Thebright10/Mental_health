Immediate Coping Techniques

While reaching out for help, these evidence-based techniques can help you feel safer right now:

🌬️
Box Breathing

Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. Activates your parasympathetic nervous system instantly.

✋
5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Anchors you to the present moment.

🧊
Temperature Shift

Hold cold water in your hands or splash your face. The temperature shift triggers the dive reflex and slows your heart rate rapidly.

🏃
Physical Movement

A short walk, jumping jacks, or stretching releases tension stored in the body and shifts nervous system state.

📝
Safety Planning

Write down 3 people you can call, 3 places you can go, and 3 reasons to stay safe. Keep this list accessible.

💬
Talk to MindFlow AI

Our AI companion is available 24/7. While not a replacement for human support, talking can help you feel less alone in the moment.

Warning Signs to Watch For

In yourself or others, these signs indicate immediate professional help is needed:

  • Talking about wanting to die or to kill yourself.
  • Looking for ways to hurt yourself — searching online or acquiring means.
  • Talking about being a burden to others or feeling hopeless or trapped.
  • Withdrawing from friends, family, and activities you used to enjoy.
  • Extreme mood swings or giving away prized possessions.
  • Saying goodbye as if you won't see someone again.

If you notice these signs, take action immediately. Call a helpline, go to the nearest emergency room, or call 112.
