Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

The gold standard for anxiety and depression. CBT helps you identify and restructure negative thought patterns, replacing distorted thinking with realistic, balanced perspectives.

Best for: Anxiety, Depression, OCD, PTSD, Phobias

12 Tips to Maximize Your Therapy

📅
Commit to Consistency

Weekly sessions are more effective than sporadic ones. Treat therapy like a non-negotiable appointment with your future self.

📓
Journal Between Sessions

Write down thoughts, emotions, and insights between sessions. Reviewing them helps you arrive with clarity and maximizes session time.

🎯
Set Clear Goals

Work with your therapist to define specific, measurable goals. Vague goals like "feel better" make progress harder to track and sustain.

💬
Be Radically Honest

Your therapist can only work with what you share. The topics you're most reluctant to discuss are often the most important ones.

🔄
Do Your Homework

Practice techniques between sessions. Research shows applying skills daily accelerates progress 3× faster than in-session work alone.

⚡
Speak Up if It's Not Working

If you feel stuck, say so. A good therapist will adjust the approach. The therapeutic relationship is a collaboration, not a one-way street.

😴
Prioritize Sleep & Exercise

Mental health improvements in therapy are amplified by adequate sleep (7–9 hrs) and regular movement. These are non-negotiable foundations.

🧘
Combine with Mindfulness

Mindfulness practice between sessions helps you notice thought patterns in real time — making therapy insights actionable in daily life.

🌱
Expect Discomfort

Therapy often feels harder before it gets better. Working through difficult emotions is necessary for healing — discomfort means growth.

Finding the Right Therapist

The therapeutic relationship is the most important predictor of success in therapy. Finding the right fit is worth the investment of time.
